# Benchmark v3 — full results (shared blind evaluator)

All numbers from ONE evaluator (`spotpipe evaluate`) over the same 55 v3 conditions: 42 SNR x density cells + 13 curvature sets. Hungarian matching at **1.68 px** (= 1.0 x max PSF sigma), unweighted OLS alpha fit, false positives binned per-stratum. No per-tool code — the fairness guarantee.

**Model:** `headfix40k-DELTA` (HRNet-large + (logI1, Delta) head, 40k steps, peak_threshold 0.3 retuned off-benchmark).  
**Baseline:** cmeAnalysis in 4 configurations — `native` = its own photometry, `shared` = our shared extraction at its positions; `given-sigma` = handed the published PSF sigma.

> **SIGN CONVENTION.** These alpha values are the **raw OLS slope** of `log(A2/A1)` vs `log(sqrt(A1))`, which is how `true_alpha` is injected. The real-data figures report the field-standard exponent **alpha = -slope** (rho ~ r^-alpha, positive alpha = positive curvature sensing). Magnitudes are identical (alpha-MAE and |null| are sign-symmetric) but **do not mix a benchmark alpha and a figure alpha in one table without converting.**

> **No >=6 px border margin was applied** (evaluator TODO). cmeAnalysis cannot fit spots near the frame edge, so this favours our padded CNN by roughly 5 F1 points. Fix before publishing detection numbers.

> **Curvature-set operating point:** density drawn log-uniform in **0.0004–0.0015 spots/px** (~26–98 spots per 256^2 image), uniform placement, no clustering. Sparser than every family-1 cell, so slope recovery is decoupled from crowding. Per-spot SNR varies within and across sets (a wide A1 range is required to fit a slope): median SNR ~5.3 at alpha=0 but ~1 at |alpha|=1.2, where the saturation-safe window forces one channel dim.
